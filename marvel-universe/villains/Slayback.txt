'''Slayback''' is a [[character (arts)|fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. Primarily an enemy of [[Deadpool]],<ref> {{cite web|url = http://screenrant.com/future-x-men-films-deadpool-magneto/|title = The Next ''X-Men'' Films Part Two: Deadpool, Magneto|last = Keyes|first = Rob|publisher = Screen Rant|date = 25 October 2009|website = screenrant.com|accessdate = 20 November 2016}} </ref> the character exists within Marvel's main [[shared universe]], known as the [[Marvel Universe]]. Created by writer [[Fabian Nicieza]] and artist [[Joe Madureira]], the character first appeared in ''Deadpool: The Circle Chase'' #1 (August 1993).<ref> {{cite book|author = Robert G. Weiner|title = Marvel Graphic Novels and Related Publications: An Annotated Guide to Comics, Prose Novels, Children's Books, Articles, Criticism and Reference Works|year = 2008|publisher = [[McFarland & Company]]|isbn = 9780786425006|page = 100}}</ref><ref> {{cite web|url = http://www.syfy.com/syfywire/celebrate-25-years-deadpool-25-merc-tastic-moments-characters-history|title = Celebrate 25 years of Deadpool with 25 merc-tastic moments from the character's history|last = Funk|first = Matthew|publisher = [[Syfy]]|date = 10 February 2016|website = syfy.com|accessdate = 20 November 2016}} </ref>

== Publication history ==

After debuting in the four-issue miniseries ''Deadpool: The Circle Chase'', Slayback went on to appear in ''Wolverine Annual 1995, Deadpool'' Vol. 3, #61-63, ''Death of Wolverine: The Weapon X Program'' #1,<ref> {{cite web|url = http://www.theouthousers.com/index.php/news/129618-really-marvel-is-wolverine-back-already-spoilers.html|title = Really, Marvel? Is Wolverine Back Already? (Spoilers)|last = Terror|first = Jude|publisher = The Outhousers|date = 7 November 2014|website = theouthousers.com|accessdate = 20 November 2016}} </ref> ''Deadpool vs. Thanos'' #3 and ''Deadpool & the Mercs for Money'' Vol. 1, #4.

== Fictional character biography ==

Claiming to have come from a wealthy and loving home, Australian-born Gregory Terraerton was at some point turned into a cyborg dubbed "Slayback" by the [[Weapon X]] Program. Slayback afterward became a mercenary and worked alongside fellow Weapon X members [[Deadpool]], [[Garrison Kane]], and Sluggo, as well as the mutant shapeshifter [[Copycat (Marvel Comics)|Copycat]]. Over time, Deadpool grew disgusted by Slayback's sociopathy and sadism and attempted to kill him by blowing him up, unaware that Slayback had regenerative abilities that, over a period of ten years, allowed him to recover from Deadpool's attack.<ref name = "CC"> {{Cite comic|writer = Fabian Nicieza|penciller = Joe Madureira|inker = Mark Farmer and Harry Candelario|colorist = Glynis Oliver|letterer = Chris Eliopoulos|editor = Suzanne Gaffney|title = Deadpool: The Circle Chase|volume = 1|issue = #1-4|date = August - November 1993|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref>

Obsessed with getting revenge on Deadpool, Slayback steals files pertaining to him from Department K and attempts to force Kane into revealing Deadpool's whereabouts. Next, Slayback, aware that Deadpool was among the mercenaries competing for [[Genesis (Marvel Comics)|Tolliver]]'s inheritance, discerns that it is located in a Nepalese temple, where he captures Copycat. When Deadpool, Kane and [[Weasel (Marvel Comics)|Weasel]] arrive to claim Tolliver's treasure, Slayback attacks them and fatally wounds Copycat. The android [[Zero (Marvel Comics)|Zero]], who had been among Tolliver's belongings, is reactivated by the battle and disintegrates Slayback.<ref name = "CC"/>

Slayback survived or was resurrected and came to work for Doctor Westergaard, who has Slayback capture Deadpool for use as a test subject for her experiments involving the [[Legacy Virus]]. Deadpool is rescued by [[Wolverine (character)|Wolverine]] and [[David North (comics)|Maverick]] while Slayback is abandoned and blown up by Westergaard.<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = Chris Golden|penciller = Ben Herrera|inker = Vince Russell|colorist = Ian Laughlin|letterer = Richard Starkings and Comicraft|editor = Mark Powers|story = What the Cat Dragged In|title = Wolverine Annual 1995|volume = 2|issue = #1|date = September 1995|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref>

Allison Kemp later hires Slayback and [[T-Ray (comics)|T-Ray]] to help her kill Deadpool.<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = Daniel Way|penciller = Ale Garza|inker = Sean Parsons|colorist = Guru eFX|letterer = VC's Joe Sabino|editor = Jordan D. White|story = The Salted Earth, Part One: Innocent of Nothing|title = Deadpool|volume = 2|issue = #61|date = 26 September 2012|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref><ref> {{Cite comic|writer = Daniel Way|penciller = Ale Garza and Matteo Lolli|inker = Sean Parsons and Don Ho|colorist = Guru eFX|letterer = VC's Joe Sabino|editor = Jordan D. White|story = The Salted Earth, Part Two: More Than Words|title = Deadpool|volume = 2|issue = #62|date = 10 October 2012|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref> When Deadpool attacks Kemp's airship, Slayback panics and attempts to flee via parachute pack, realizing too late that Deadpool had stuffed it full of explosives which detonate in mid-air as Slayback screams, "Oh, fu-".<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = Daniel Way|penciller = Filipe Andrade|inker = Sean Parsons and Jeff Huet|colorist = Guru eFX|letterer = VC's Joe Sabino|editor = Jordan D. White|story = The Salted Earth, Part Three: Conclusion|title = Deadpool|volume = 2|issue = #63|date = 24 October 2012|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref> Slayback is subsequently shown running amok in Hell during a period of cosmic imbalance caused by [[Death (Marvel Comics)|Death]] being imprisoned by [[Eternity (comics)|Eternity]].<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = Tim Seeley|penciller = Elmo Bondoc|inker = Elmo Bondoc|colorist = Ruth Redmond|letterer = VC's Joe Sabino|editor = Jordan D. White|story = Part Three|title = Deadpool vs. Thanos|volume = 1|issue = #3|date = 14 October 2015|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref>

A revived Slayback rejoins Weapon X and is seemingly killed yet again when a group of the organization's escaped test subjects stab and immolate him during ''[[Death of Wolverine]]''.<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = Charles Soule|penciller = Salvador Larroca|inker = Salvador Larroca|colorist = Frank D'Armata|letterer = VC's Cory Petit|editor = Katie Kubert and Mike Marts|story = Phase One: Question|title = Death of Wolverine: The Weapon X Program|volume = 1|issue = #1|date = 5 November 2014|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref> Slayback afterward appears as one of the villains vying for the Rigellian Recorder acquired by Deadpool and the [[Mercs for Money]]. When questioned about his return by an annoyed Deadpool, Slayback merely quips, "The kind of money being offered for killing you... is worth crawling outta the grave for!"<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = Cullen Bunn|penciller = Salva Espin|inker = Salva Espin|colorist = Guru-eFX|letterer = VC's Joe Sabino|editor = Jordan D. White|title = Deadpool & the Mercs for Money|volume = 1|issue = #4|date = 18 May 2016|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref>

== Powers and abilities ==

Physical and bionic augmentations provided by [[Weapon X]] have imbued Slayback with superhuman strength and durability, a healing factor, telescopic arms and enlargeable hands and fingers that can morph into razor sharp spikes and talons, respectively.
