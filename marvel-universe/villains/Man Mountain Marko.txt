'''Man Mountain Marko''' ('''Michael Marko''') is a [[Marvel Comics]] supervillain. Marko was and remains an affiliate of numerous [[Features of the Marvel Universe#Criminal organizations|organized-crime entities]] in the [[Marvel Comics|Marvel]] universe, including the [[Maggia (comics)|Maggia]].

He was allied with [[Silvermane]], Caesar Cicero, [[Eel (comics)|Eel]] I, and [[Nightshade (Marvel Comics)|Nightshade]]. His most frequent enemies are [[Spider-Man]], [[Luke Cage|Power Man]], [[Iron Fist (comics)|Iron Fist]], [[Thunderbolt (Marvel Comics)|Thunderbolt]], and [[Boomerang (comics)|Boomerang]]. He appears to have no relation to Cain Marko, seen in various Marvel titles as the [[Juggernaut (comics)|Juggernaut]], despite similarities in surname, physique and superpowers.

==Publication history==
Man Mountain Marko first appeared in ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man]]'' #73 and created by [[Stan Lee]] and [[John Romita Sr.]]

==Fictional character biography==
Marko is first seen as [[Silvermane (comics)|Silvermane]]'s loyal lieutenant in an incident dealing with an ancient tablet which he steals. He attacks Conner when he thinks the formula he created has killed Silvermane. Marko is quickly defeated by Spider-Man.<ref>{{cite book|last = Manning|first = Matthew K.|last2= Gilbert|first2= Laura, ed.|chapter= 1960s|title = Spider-Man Chronicle Celebrating 50 Years of Web-Slinging|publisher = [[Dorling Kindersley]]|year = 2012|page = 48|isbn = 978-0756692360|quote= Spider-Man called the Shocker's ex-girlfriend, only to find her home under siege by a giant thug named Man Mountain Marko.}}</ref><ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #73-75 (June–August 1969)</ref>

Marko and some of his colleagues rob the Debutante Ball of Millie Hogarth. Unfortunately, for them, Millie's father is the agent for [[Luke Cage|Power Man]] and [[Iron Fist (comics)|Iron Fist]], who were called in to defeat the villains. Man Mountain Marko later assisted Caesar Cicero's men into attacking Luke Cage and [[Big Ben Donovan]].<ref>''Power Man and Iron Fist'' #61-62, 92, 110 (February 1980, April 1980, April 1983, October 1983)</ref>

During a trip to gather protection money, Marko has some sort of breakdown and trashes an entire bar, with Spider-Man inside of it. Investigative reporter [[Ben Urich]] tells Spider-Man that Marko has apparently also kidnapped a child. Ben knows of a facility where Marko is known to work out. Spider-Man finds steroids in Marko's locker. The owner of the gym tells him that the Maggia genetically altered Marko in order to make him stronger. Spider-Man steals information from the Maggia and confronts Marko at his home. Eventually Marko is subdued and it turns out the kid he 'kidnapped' simply had a twisted sense of hero worship, admiring Marko's size and strength.<ref>''Web of Spider-Man'' #82 (October 1984)</ref>

Man Mountain Marko is later seen as a sexually threatening hitman sent after private investigator [[Jessica Jones]].<ref>''Alias'' #4</ref><ref>''Alias'' #5</ref> This was part of a conspiracy by a powerful business magnate who wished to use Jones and [[Captain America]] as a way to humiliate the current President of the United States. Jones severely beats Marko, then literally throws him at the businessman's feet. Marko and his bosses are soon arrested by [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]]<ref>''Alias'' #5 (March 2002)</ref>

During the "[[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]]" storyline, Man Mountain Marko was visible among an army of super-villains organized by [[Hammerhead (comics)|Hammerhead]] that was captured by [[Iron Man]] and S.H.I.E.L.D. agents.<ref>''[[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]]: War Crimes'' #1 (February 2007)</ref>

During the "[[X-Men: Manifest Destiny|Manifest Destiny]]" storyline, Marko develops a singing career focusing on the concept of violence against female super-heroes. While on a plane with the super-hero [[Dazzler (Marvel Comics)|Dazzler]] he becomes enraged over the perceived lack of alcohol. He injures passengers and takes a hostage, he breaks the hostage's wrist to prove he is serious. Dazzler, at low power, manages to subdue him.<ref>''X-Men: Manifest Destiny'' #5 (Jun 2009)</ref>

During the "[[Dark Reign (comics)|Dark Reign]]" storyline, Marko is later sent by [[Green Goblin|Norman Osborn]] as part of a fact finding mission to an 'Atlas' facility run by a younger [[Jimmy Woo]]. Atlas is an international crime organization that is working against the government's interests. Marko is working as the head of a squad of [[Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|B.A.T.F.E.]] government agents. Marko's forces including a seeming snitch that had been advising the government on the activities of Atlas. Marko, against the recommendation Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosivess of his guides, rushes off the recommended path to follow the snitch. The man is really Jimmy Woo. Before Marko can do anything about the situation, he is eaten by a sentient dragon named Lao. This was against Woo's wishes.<ref>''Agents of Atlas'' Vol. 2 #1 (April 2009)</ref> Marko is replaced by another super-powered strongman, the [[Grizzly (comics)|Grizzly]].<ref>''Agents of Atlas'' Vol. 2 #3 (June 2009)</ref>

Marko is revealed to have somehow survived when he is hired onto the [[Sinister Six]]teen by [[Boomerang (comics)|Boomerang]] and [[Owl (Marvel Comics)|Owl]].<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Nick Spencer|penciller = Steve Lieber|inker = Steve Lieber|colorist = Rachelle Rosenberg|letterer = VC's Clayton Cowles|editor = Lauren Sankovitch|story = Hammerhead of the Family|title = The Superior Foes of Spider-Man|volume = 1|issue = #12|date = 4 June 2014|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref> After being manipulated and abandoned by Boomerang, Marko seeks revenge on him with the help [[Cyclone (Marvel Comics)|Cyclone]], [[Shriek (comics)|Shriek]], and [[Kangaroo (comics)|Kangaroo]], but the quartet are defeated by Boomerang and his allies [[Janice Lincoln|Beetle]], [[Speed Demon (comics)|Speed Demon]], and [[Overdrive (comics)|Overdrive]].<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Nick Spencer|penciller = Steve Lieber|inker = Steve Lieber|colorist = Rachelle Rosenberg|letterer = VC's Clayton Cowles|editor = Lauren Sankovitch|story = Something to Worry About|title = The Superior Foes of Spider-Man|volume = 1|issue = #15|date = 3 September 2014|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref>

As part of the "[[All-New, All-Different Marvel]]," Marko is hired by Lorraine Monroe to stand guard over Tempest Monroe, the comatose fiancé of [[Spider-Man 2099]]. Upon discovering Tempest's whereabouts, Spider-Man 2099 distracts Marko long enough for [[Parker Industries]] to covertly relocate Tempest.<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Peter David|penciller = [[Will Sliney]]|inker = Will Sliney|colorist = Rachelle Rosenberg|letterer = VC's Cory Petit|editor = Devin Lewis|title = Spider-Man 2099|volume = 3|issue = #8|date = 9 March 2016|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref> Spider-Man 2099 subsequently tracks the escaped Man Mountain Marko down and beats him to near-death while demanding to know where to find Lorraine.<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Peter David|penciller = Will Sliney|inker = Will Sliney|colorist = Rachelle Rosenberg|letterer = VC's Cory Petit|editor = Devin Lewis and Charles Beacham|title = Spider-Man 2099|volume = 3|issue = #9|date = 6 April 2016|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref> Marko is recovered by the Fist, an offshoot of the [[The Hand (comics)|Hand]] which heals him and further augments his strength and durability using technology provided by [[Tyler Stone]]. Tyler Stone later has Marko ambush Spider-Man 2099 while he absconds with the recovered Tempest.<ref name = "SM20">{{Cite comic|writer = Peter David|penciller = Will Sliney|inker = Will Sliney|colorist = Rachelle Rosenberg|letterer = VC's Cory Petit|editor = Charles Beacham|title = Spider-Man 2099|volume = 3|issue = #20|date = 1 February 2017|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref>

During the "[[Civil War II]]" storyline, Man Mountain Marko worked with [[Kingpin (character)|Kingpin]]'s former minion Janus Jardeesh in the human trafficking business until they encounter Kingpin and [[Turk Barrett]].<ref>''Civil War II: Kingpin'' #1</ref>

Marko later appears with Speed Demon robbing a pawn shop until they were caught by [[Rage (comics)|Rage]]. After a brief fight, they escape while Rage gets arrested by the [[Americop]]s.<ref>''Captain America: Sam Wilson'' #17</ref> [[Falcon (comics)|Captain America]] later caught Speed Demon where he confessed to his and Man Mountain Marko's robbery of the pawn shop.<ref>''Captain America: Sam Wilson'' #19</ref>

Marko and [[Ringer (comics)|Ringer]] attack a book fair to rob it only to be defeated by Spider-Man.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' Vol. 5 #2. Marvel Comics.</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Man Mountain Marko has been shown to have superhuman strength, stamina and sturdiness roughly comparable to, if not slightly exceeding, those of [[Spider-Man]]. It has been said that this is a result of steroids and genetic manipulation. He received a power upgrade from Tyler Stone after getting a vicious beating from Spider-Man 2099.  Though Spider-Man had previously broken many of Marko's bones, afterward Spider-Man was unable to do any damage to Marko.<ref name = "SM20"/>

==Relatives==

A '''Mick "Mountain" Marko''' appears in ''Cage [[Max (comics)|MAX]]'' as a thug [[Tombstone (comics)|Tombstone]] hires to assault Luke Cage.<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Brian Azzarello|penciller = Richard Corben|inker = Richard Corben|colorist = Jose Villarrubia|letterer = RS and Comicraft's Wes Abbott|editor = Axel Alonso|story = Cage, Part Four|title = Cage|volume = 2|issue = #4|date = August 2002|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref> After recovering from the attack, Cage pummels Mick and chains him to a street corner where he is shot to death by one of Tombstone's rivals.<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Brian Azzarello|penciller = Richard Corben|inker = Richard Corben|colorist = Jose Villarrubia|letterer = RS and Comicraft's Wes Abbott|editor = Axel Alonso|story = Cage, Part Five|title = Cage|volume = 2|issue = #5|date = September 2002|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref>

During the ''[[Fear Itself (comics)|Fear Itself]]'' storyline, it is revealed that Man Mountain Marko had a cousin named '''Man Mountain Mario''' who was an inmate at the Raft. After the destruction of the Raft prison by [[Juggernaut (comics)|Juggernaut]], Man Mountain Mario helps defend Crossbones from thugs. While the two of them were trying to escape, Mario told Crossbones about his grandma who helps criminals leave the border. Crossbones manages to escape from the Raft and returned the favor for Mario helping him by killing him to help with his escape.<ref>''Thunderbolts'' #159 (June 2011)</ref>

==Other versions==

===MC2===
The [[Marvel Comics 2]] universe shows that Marko is still active in the [[Marvel Comics 2|MC2]] universe, and is in charge of the Maggia of New York.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Girl'' #17</ref> He later appears in the new ''Spectacular Spider-Girl'' series as the underling for the new Maggia Crime boss of New York.<ref>''Spectacular Spider-Girl'' Vol. 1 #2</ref>

==In other media==

===Television===
* Man Mountain Marko appeared in the 1981 ''[[Spider-Man (1981 TV series)|Spider-Man]]'' episode "Wrath of the Sub-Mariner" voiced by [[Jack Angel]]. He is shown to be [[Silvermane]]'s henchman and was with him when Spider-Man stopped Silvermane's limo resulting in a brief fight between Spider-Man and Man Mountain Marko. He was the first to notice Spider-Man at Kingpin's meeting with Silvermane, [[Hammerhead (comics)|Hammerhead]], and Caesar Cicero on a yacht. Man Mountain Marko was easily defeated by [[Namor]] and later webbed by Spider-Man.
