'''Bullet''' is a [[character (arts)|fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. Existing within Marvel's main [[shared universe]], known as the [[Marvel Universe]]. Bullet first appeared in the pages of ''[[Daredevil (Marvel Comics series)|Daredevil]]'' in 1988, created by [[John Romita Jr.]] and [[Ann Nocenti]].

==Fictional character biography==
'''Bullet (Buck Cashman)''' is a mercenary who serves as an agent of the United States government, assigned to perform covert activities. He is directed by government officials to assist in terrorist activities against environmentalists, on the behalf of [[Kingpin (character)|Wilson Fisk]]. He first comes into conflict with [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]] and battles him on this mission.<ref>''Daredevil'' #250</ref>

Bullet also participates in an attack along with [[Typhoid Mary (comics)|Typhoid Mary]] and other criminals that nearly results in the death of Daredevil.<ref>''Daredevil'' #259-260</ref>

Bullet next battles Daredevil again, mistakenly thinking Daredevil is a threat to his son, Lance.<ref>''Daredevil'' #267</ref>

Some time later, the Kingpin sent Bullet to retrieve a contractor's map, but Bullet encountered Daredevil along the way, who defeated him.<ref>''Daredevil'' #291</ref> Bullet was later among the superhuman criminals who tried to escape from the [[Vault (comics)|Vault]], a prison for superhumans. Along with [[Griffin (Marvel Comics)|Griffin]] and [[Orka (comics)|Orka]] he makes it farther than most, emerging from a vent half a mile from the prison. All three are defeated by [[Hank Pym]] and [[Captain America]].<ref>''Vault Graphic Novel''</ref>

Bullet is later seen being beaten by Daredevil during Matt's year as Kingpin of [[Hell's Kitchen, Manhattan|Hell's Kitchen]].<ref>''Daredevil'' vol. 2 #71</ref>

Sometime later, Bullet is among a group of contract killers hired by the Assassins' Guild to hunt [[Wolverine (character)|Wolverine]] and [[Domino (comics)|Domino]] of [[X-Force]]. During the ensuing battle, Bullet is stabbed in the chest by [[Warpath (comics)|Warpath]].<ref>''X-Force: Sex and Violence'' #3</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Bullet appears to have near superhuman strength, speed, stamina, and durability. He also appears to have an immunity to toxic chemical waste.

Bullet has received hand-to-hand combat training from the U.S. government.

Bullet is also proficient in weaponry and tactics.
