The '''Ogress''' ('''Diane Davids''') is a [[fictional character]] that appears in [[comic books]] published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
The Ogress first appeared in ''Incredible Hulk'' #345 (July 1988), and was created by [[Peter David]] and [[Todd McFarlane]].

The character subsequently appears in ''Incredible Hulk'' #366 (February 1990), #397-398 (September–October 1992), #400 (December 1992), #439-440 (March–April 1996), and ''Avengers'' #397 (April 1996).

==Fictional character biography==
Diane Davids was a criminal defense lawyer and a resident of Middletown, an isolated desert town, which the [[Hulk]]'s archenemy [[Leader (comics)|Leader]] chooses as the target of an experiment in which he exposes it to gamma radiation. He hopes some of its inhabitants would survive and join him in his new kingdom of Freehold.<ref>[[David, Peter]] (w), [[McFarlane, Todd]] (a). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #345. Marvel Comics.</ref> Diane was one of only five people to survive a gamma explosion and develop superhuman powers. Diane becomes Ogress and joins the other four people into forming the [[Riot Squad (comics)|Riot Squad]], which protects Leader's Freehold base at the time when Leader had contacted a terminal illness. They first clash with Bruce Banner/Hulk shortly before the Leader provides him the location of Bruce Banner's wife, [[Betty Ross]] in exchange for Hulk killing Leader's brother, [[Madman (Marvel Comics)|Madman]].<ref>David, Peter (w). [[Purves, Jeff]] (p). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #366. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Ogress is a member of the Riot Squad when they help the [[U-Foes]] distract Hulk and the [[Pantheon (Marvel Comics)|Pantheon]] at the time when Leader was meets with [[Agamemnon (comics)|Agamemnon]].<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #397. Marvel Comics.</ref> Hulk and the Pantheon's fight with the U-Foes and the Riot Squad are broken up when Agamemnon reaches an agreement with Leader.<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk #398. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Ogress was present with the Riot Squad at the time when [[Hydra (comics)|HYDRA]] invades Leader's base with the resulting conflict causing the death of Soul Man (who was in the middle of reviving [[Marlo Chandler]]).<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #400. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Ogress and Hotshot fight Hulk at the time when Omnibus plans to cause destruction throughout the world and they end up defeated.<ref>''The Incredible Hulk'' #439. Marvel Comics.</ref> Ogress was among those with Hotshot when they storm Omnibus' lair and accuse him of being behind the Alliance before taking him away.<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #440. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Ogress and the rest of the Riot Squad worked with the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] to shut down the remaining bombs. The Riot Squad then judges Omnibus to be guilty of being behind the Alliance and end up sentencing Omnibus to death.<ref>''Avengers'' #397. Marvel Comics.</ref> Ogress and the rest of the Riot Squad left Omnibus in the Arctic to die.<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #442. Marvel Comics.</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Ogress possesses superhuman strength and durability rivaling that of the Hulk.{{citation needed|date=May 2018}}

==In other media==
Ogress appears in ''[[The Incredible Hulk (1996 TV series)|The Incredible Hulk]]'' TV series voiced by [[Kathy Ireland]]. She is one of the Leader's minions and leads his army of Gamma Warriors cloned from Hulk's DNA.{{episode needed|date=May 2018}}
