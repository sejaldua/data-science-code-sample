The '''Iron Maiden''' ('''Melina Von Vostokoff''') is a [[fictional]] [[supervillain]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. She first appears in ''[[Marvel Fanfare]]'' #11 (November 1983) and was created by [[Ralph Macchio (comics)|Ralph Macchio]] and [[George Pérez]]. The character is [[Russia]]n and is depicted most notably an enemy of the [[Black Widow (Natalia Romanova)|Black Widow]]. She was a member of the [[Thunderbolts]] and [[Femizons]].

[[Rachel Weisz]] will portray Melina Vostokoff in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] film ''[[Black Widow (2020 film)|Black Widow]]''.

==Publication history==
{{expand-section|date=July 2019}}
Iron Maiden first appeared in ''[[Marvel Fanfare]]'' #11 (November 1983), created by [[Ralph Macchio (comics)|Ralph Macchio]] and [[George Pérez]].

==Fictional character biography==
Hired as an assassin for the [[Russian government]], Iron Maiden fought [[Black Widow (Natalia Romanova)|Black Widow]] until [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] agents arrived and put an end to the battle.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Marvel Fanfare|volume=1|issue=#11-12|writer=[[Ralph Macchio (comics)|Ralph Macchio]], [[George Pérez]]|artist=George Pérez|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=January - March, 1984}}</ref> She then joined the [[Femizons]] and became one of [[Superia]]'s lieutenants.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Captain America|volume=1|issue=#389|writer=[[Mark Gruenwald]]|artist=[[Rik Levins]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=August, 1991}}</ref>

She was eventually apprehended and coerced into joining the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]] during ''[[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]]''.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Thunderbolts|volume=1|issue=#105|writer=[[Fabian Nicieza]]|artist=[[Tom Grummett]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=October, 2006}}</ref>

She later joined the Soviet Revolutionaries Remont Six and led them in an attack on an [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|AIM]] base outside the Forbidden Zone in Russia. Although her team overpowered [[Red Guardian]] and [[Crimson Dynamo]] with ease, [[Ursa Major (comics)|Ursa Major]] was able to knock most of them out.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Hulk: Winter Guard|volume=1|issue=#1|writer=[[David Gallaher]]|artist=[[Steve Ellis (comics)|Steve Ellis]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=December 3, 2009}}</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Iron Maiden is a master assassin, horticulturalist and spy. She wears a lightweight but strong metal suit that protects her from impacts, bullets and energy weapons. It appears to function as a form of exoskeleton, enhancing her strength to an unknown degree.

==Other versions==
===Ultimate Marvel===
Iron Maiden was a considered codename for the [[Black Widow (Natalia Romanova)#Ultimate Black Widow|Black Widow]] when she was given a black suit of Iron Man armor by [[Ultimate Iron Man (comics)|Iron Man]] as a possible wedding gift.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Ultimates 2|volume=1|issue=#4|writer=[[Mark Millar]]|artist=[[Bryan Hitch]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=May, 2005}}</ref>

===Mangaverse===
Iron Maiden is the superhero name of Antoinette "Toni" Stark ([[Iron Man|Tony Stark]]'s sister).<ref>{{cite comic|title=Marvel Mangaverse: New Dawn|volume=1|issue=#1|writer=[[Ben Dunn]]|artist=Ben Dunn|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=March, 2002}}</ref>

===Earth X===
A character with the same name plays a major role in the ''[[Earth X]]'' trilogy. She was a new character to this universe, without a known Earth-616 counterpart. A former employee of [[Mister Fantastic|Reed Richards]], she became encased in silver acid and was able to manipulate this armor into weapons. She was a servant of the [[Red Skull]] before his death, and was later revealed to have the ability to manipulate all of the Earth's vibranium.{{Issue|date=July 2019}}
