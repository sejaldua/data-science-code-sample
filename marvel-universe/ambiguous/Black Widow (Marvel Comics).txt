'''Black Widow''' is the name of several [[fictional]] characters appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. Most of these versions exist in Marvel's main [[shared universe]], known as the [[Marvel Universe]].

==Claire Voyant==
{{main|Black Widow (Claire Voyant)}}
'''Claire Voyant''' is the first costumed, superpowered female protagonist in comic books. Created by writer [[George Kapitan]] and artist [[Harry Sahle]], she first appeared in ''[[Mystic Comics]]'' #4 (Aug. 1940). She kills evildoers to deliver their souls to [[Satan]], her master. The character is unrelated to the later Marvel Comics superheroines who took on the codename.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://screenrant.com/black-widow-body-trivia-facts/|title=20 Weird Things About Black Widow Even Hardcore Fans Might Not Know - 3|last=Gerber|first=Jamie|publisher=[[ScreenRant]]|date=Jun 27, 2018|accessdate=May 29, 2019|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190203203421/https://screenrant.com/black-widow-body-trivia-facts/|archivedate=February 3, 2019}}</ref>

==Natalia Romanova / Natasha Romanoff==
{{main|Black Widow (Natasha Romanova)}}

'''Natalia "Natasha" Alianovna Romanova / Natasha Romanoff'''<ref>{{cite comic|title=[[Wolverine: Origins]]|volume=1|issue=16|writer=[[Daniel Way]]|artist=[[Steve Dillon]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=October, 2007}}</ref> is the first character to take on the Black Widow codename in the modern mainstream Marvel Comics. She was created by editor and plotter [[Stan Lee]], scripter [[Don Rico]] and artist [[Don Heck]], and first appeared in ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #52 (April 1964). The character has been associated with several superhero teams in the Marvel Universe, including the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]], the [[Champions (1975 team)|Champions]], [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]], and the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]]. She has appeared in many other forms of media, including the major motion pictures ''[[Iron Man 2]]'', ''[[The Avengers (2012 film)|The Avengers]]'', ''[[Captain America: The Winter Soldier]]'', ''[[Avengers: Age of Ultron]]'', ''[[Captain America: Civil War]]'', ''[[Avengers: Infinity War]]'', ''[[Avengers: Endgame]]'', and the [[Black Widow (2020 film)|upcoming solo prequel film]], wherein she is portrayed by actress [[Scarlett Johansson]].

==Yelena Belova==
{{main|Black Widow (Yelena Belova)}}

'''Yelena Belova''' is the second character to take on the Black Widow codename in the modern mainstream comics who debuted briefly in ''[[Inhumans]]'' #5 (March 1999) and was fully introduced in the 1999 [[Marvel Knights]] mini-series ''Black Widow''. A second miniseries, also titled ''Black Widow'' and featuring Natasha Romanoff and [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]], followed in 2001. The next year, she did a solo turn in her own three-issue miniseries titled ''Black Widow: Pale Little Spider'' under the mature-audience [[Marvel MAX]] imprint. This June to August 2002 story arc, by writer [[Greg Rucka]] and artist [[Igor Kordey]], was a [[Flashback (narrative)|flashback]] to the story of her becoming the second modern 'Black Widow', in events preceding her ''Inhumans'' appearance.<ref name=""CBRrevisit">{{cite web|url=https://www.cbr.com/revisiting-marvels-beezer-belova/|title=Revisiting Marvel's Beezer &amp; Belova|last=De Blieck Jr.|first=Augie|publisher=[[Comic Book Resources]]|date= July 16, 2013|accessdate=June 22, 2019|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190616051339/https://www.cbr.com/revisiting-marvels-beezer-belova/|archivedate=June 16, 2019}}</ref>

====Fictional character biography====
'''Monica Chang-Fury''' is the second character to use the Black Widow codename in the [[Ultimate Marvel]] continuity, debuting in ''[[Ultimate Comics: Avengers]]'' #3.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://comicbook.com/2014/11/01/could-the-avengers-age-of-ultron-mystery-woman-be-avengers-ais-m/|title=Could the Avengers: Age of Ultron Mystery Woman Be Avengers AI's Monica Chang?|last=Burlingame|first=Russ|publisher=Comicbook.com|date=November 2, 2014|accessdate=May 29, 2019|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170927170804/https://comicbook.com/2014/11/01/could-the-avengers-age-of-ultron-mystery-woman-be-avengers-ais-m/|archivedate=September 27, 2017}}</ref>

Despite the painful memories associated with the previous Black Widow (Natasha Romanoff), she refuses to change her codename. It is revealed that Monica is Asian-American and was [[Nick Fury (Ultimate Marvel character)|Nick Fury]]'s ex-wife after the marriage fell apart after she discovered that Nick had been sleeping with various female members of her family and limited social circle, including her own mother. With the Avengers, she and others led a mission to re-capture [[Captain America (Ultimate Marvel character)|Captain America]] and then ends up facing and fighting against the [[Red Skull]].<ref>{{cite comic|title=[[Ultimate Comics Avengers]]|volume=1|issue=#1-6|writer=[[Mark Millar]]|artist=[[Carlos Pacheco]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=August, 2009 - April, 2010}}</ref>

She also helps capture and recruit the [[Punisher]] into the team.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Ultimate Comics Avengers 2|volume=1|issue=#1|writer=[[Mark Millar]]|artist=[[Leinil Francis Yu]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=April 2010}}</ref> Later she was transferred to the New Ultimates.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Ultimate Avengers vs. New Ultimates|volume=1|issue=#1|writer=[[Mark Millar]]|artist=[[Leinil Yu]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=February, 2011}}</ref> After Fury returned as director of S.H.I.E.L.D., he reassembled the Ultimates and Monica rejoined that team, moving with her and Fury's child, Julius Chang, to the Triskelion.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Ultimate Comics Ultimates|volume=1|issue=#3|writer=[[Jonathan Hickman]]|artist=[[Esad Ribic]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=October, 2011}}</ref> She then became director of S.H.I.E.L.D.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Ultimate Comics: Ultimates|volume=1|issue=#21|writer=[[Sam Humphries]]|artist=[[Scot Eaton]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=February, 2013}}</ref>

In the ''All-New Ultimates'' final series, Monica tells Jessica Drew after they captured [[Crossbones (comics)|Crossbones]].<ref name="allnew12">{{cite comic|title=All-New Ultimates|volume=1|issue=#12|writer=[[Michel Fiffe]]|artist=Amilcar Pinna|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=January 2015}}</ref> Later, while working with the [[FBI]], Monica apparently is killed by [[Norman Osborn|Green Goblin]].<ref>{{cite comic|title=Miles Morales: Ultimate Spider-Man|volume=1|issue=#3|writer=Brian Michael Bendis|artist=[[David Marquez (comics)|David Marquez]]|date=July, 2014}}</ref>

===Black Widow 2099===
The futuristic [[Marvel 2099|2099]] version of Black Widow is an African-American woman named '''Tania'''. She operates as part of the Avengers 2099 at the [[Alchemax]] corporation's behest.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Secret Wars 2099|volume=#1|issue=|writer=[[Peter David]]|artist=[[Will Sliney]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=May, 2015}}</ref> Like black widow spiders, she literally eats her mates after having sex with them.<ref>{{cite comic|title=Secret Wars 2099|volume=1|issue=#3|writer=[[Peter David]]|artist=[[Will Sliney]]|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]|date=July, 2015}}</ref>

===Dottie Underwood===
