'''Eden Fesi''', also known as '''Manifold''', is a [[fictional character|fictional]], [[Mutant (Marvel Comics)|mutant]] [[superhero]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. Created by [[Jonathan Hickman]] and [[Stefano Caselli]], the character first appeared in ''[[Secret Warriors (comic book)|Secret Warriors]]'' #4 (July 2009), and joined that comic's regular cast. Fesi is an [[Aboriginal Australians|Aboriginal Australian]] mutant with the ability to bend time and space, connecting one piece to another and allowing him to [[Teleportation|teleport]]. Fesi joined the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] as a part of the [[Marvel NOW!]] relaunch. In 2013, [[ComicsAlliance]] ranked Manifold as #12 on their list of the "50 Sexiest Male Characters in Comics".<ref>{{cite web|last=Wheeler |first=Andrew |url=http://comicsalliance.com/comics-sexiest-male-characters |title=ComicsAlliance Presents The 50 Sexiest Male Characters in Comics |publisher=[[ComicsAlliance]] |date=2013-02-14 |accessdate=2015-07-28 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151018024021/http://comicsalliance.com/comics-sexiest-male-characters |archivedate=2015-10-18 |df= }}</ref>

==Publication history==
Eden Fesi first appeared in ''[[Secret Warriors (comic book)|Secret Warriors]]'' #4 (July 2009), and was created by writer [[Jonathan Hickman]] and artist [[Stefano Caselli]]. He appeared in the book until its conclusion in issue #28 (September 2011). He also made guest appearances as a member of the Secret Warriors in ''[[Dark Avengers]]'' #9 (November 2009), ''[[The New Avengers (comics)|New Avengers]]'' #62 (April 2010), and ''[[Siege (comics)|Siege]]'' #2-3 (April–May 2010).

Fesi, as Manifold, appears as a member of Hickman's [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], beginning with ''Avengers'' vol. 5, #1 (December 2012), a part of the [[Marvel NOW!]] relaunch. Hickman said that, when creating the character, he had initially intended to give him the codename "Calabi–Yau", named after a [[Calabi–Yau manifold]], but when adding the character to the Avengers roster he decided on the codename "Manifold" instead.<ref name="Calabi–Yau" />

==Fictional character biography==
Eden Fesi, a mutant [[Teleportation|teleporter]] living in [[Kata Tjuta]], Australia under the tutelage of [[Gateway (comics)|Gateway]], is recruited by [[Nick Fury]] to be a part of his [[Secret Warriors (Team White)|Secret Warriors]]. Hesitant at first, he agrees when Gateway gives his blessing.<ref name="SecretWarriors4" /> Almost immediately after being recruited, Eden has to teleport the entire team into the middle of a large battle aboard a dock to save Fury.<ref name="SecretWarriors6" /> He accompanies the team on several missions, including an infiltration of the [[H.A.M.M.E.R.]] base that brings them into conflict with the [[Dark Avengers]]<ref name="SecretWarriors8" /> and an attack on a [[Hydra (comics)|HYDRA]] base that leaves Eden critically wounded.<ref name="SecretWarriors20" /> The team disbands, as [[Hellfire (J.T. Slade)|Hellfire]] had betrayed them and Eden slips into a coma.<ref name="SecretWarriors23" /> Eden eventually makes a full recovery.<ref name="SecretWarriors28" />

As part of the ''[[Marvel NOW!]]'' event, Eden is now operating under the moniker Manifold. He is recruited by [[Captain America]] and [[Iron Man]] to the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], as a part of their attempt to make the team more global in scope.<ref name="AvengersV5NO1" /> As part of the Avengers, he encounters the [[Guardians of the Galaxy (2008 team)|Guardians of the Galaxy]] when the latter team is ambushed and nearly slain by mysterious aliens.<ref>''Guardians Team Up'' #1-2 (May 2015)</ref>

During the ''[[Secret Wars (2015 comic book)|Secret Wars]]'' storyline, [[Black Panther (comics)|Black Panther]] prepares Manifold for his task which calls on him to sacrifice himself to teleport other heroes to the safety of [[Mister Fantastic]]'s lifeboat. Manifold teleports other superheroes like [[Spider-Man]] and [[Star-Lord]] to Mister Fantastic's lifeboat.<ref>''Secret Wars'' #1</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Eden's mutation allows him to bend reality, folding and twisting space-time. [[Daisy Johnson]] described him as a "souped-up [[Teleportation|teleporter]]."<ref name="SecretWarriors4" /> Eden's portals stay open until he chooses to close them.

==Other versions==
In an unnamed alternate reality, Caucasian version of Manifold (wearing the same costume as Eden) appears in [[Wakanda]] alongside the villainess [[Black Swan (comics)|Black Swan]]. Black Swan later kills this Manifold after his teleportation abilities cease to function.<ref>''New Avengers'' (vol. 3) #1</ref> In the following issue, she refers to her actions as a [[mercy killing]], stating that a Manifold is unable to use his or her abilities once they leave their own [[Multiverse (Marvel Comics)|universe]].<ref>''New Avengers'' (vol. 3) #2</ref>

==In other media==

===Television===
Eden is alluded to in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] television series ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'', in the episode "The Inside Man". While the team is spying on the various foreign delegations, it is revealed that the Australian government has Eden in their possession. He is said to be an Inhuman instead of being a mutant.

===Video games===
Eden Fesi's Manifold appearance appears in ''[[Marvel Heroes (video game)|Marvel Heroes]]''.
