'''Margaret''' "'''Peggy'''" '''Carter''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. She is usually depicted as a supporting character in books featuring [[Captain America]]. Created by writer [[Stan Lee]] and artist [[Jack Kirby]], she first appeared in ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #77 as a [[World War II]] love interest of Steve Rogers in [[Flashback (narrative)|flashback]] sequences. She would later be better known as a relative of Captain America's modern-day significant other [[Sharon Carter]].

[[Hayley Atwell]] portrayed the character in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]], beginning with ''[[Captain America: The First Avenger]]'' (2011), and continuing in the [[Marvel One-Shot]] ''[[Agent Carter (film)|Agent Carter]]'', ''[[Captain America: The Winter Soldier]]'' (2014), the television series ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.|Marvel's Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'' and ''[[Agent Carter (TV series)|Marvel's Agent Carter]]'', ''[[Avengers: Age of Ultron]]'' (2015), ''[[Ant-Man (film)|Ant-Man]]'' (2015), and ''[[Avengers: Endgame]]'' (2019). Atwell will return to voice an alternate version of the character called Captain Britain in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] animated series ''[[What If (comics)|What If...?]]'' on Disney+, which will debut in mid 2021.

==Publication history==
The character first appeared, unnamed, as a wartime love interest of Captain America in ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #75 (single panel) and #77 (May 1966), by writer [[Stan Lee]] and artist [[Jack Kirby]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ign.com/characters/peggy-carter | title=Peggy Carter | publisher=[[IGN]] | accessdate=21 December 2013}}</ref><ref>[http://www.comics.org/issue/20053/#147873 "If a Hostage Should Die!"], ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #77 (May 1966) at the [[Grand Comics Database]]</ref> She appeared again as the older sister of [[Sharon Carter]] in ''[[Captain America (comic book)|Captain America]]'' #161 (May 1973). She was later [[retcon]]ned as Sharon's aunt due to the [[floating timeline|unaging nature]] of comic book characters (see ''Captain America'' Vol. 5 #25 (April 2007)). The character has appeared frequently in Captain America stories set during [[World War II]].{{citation needed|date=February 2014}}

==Fictional character biography==
Peggy Carter joins the [[French Resistance]] as a teenager and becomes a skilled fighter, who serves on several operations alongside Captain America.<ref>Catherine Saunders, Heather Scott, Julia March, and Alastair Dougall, editors, 2008, ''Marvel Chronicle: A year by Year History'', London: Dorling Kindersley, p. 115; {{ISBN|978-1-4093-8399-4}}.</ref> The two fall in love, but an exploding shell gives her amnesia and she is sent to live with her parents in Virginia.<ref>''Tales of Suspense'' #77. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the "[[Original Sin (comics)|Original Sin]]" storyline, it was revealed in 1952 that Peggy Carter worked with [[Howard Stark]] and Woody McCord when they investigated an alien ship in Siberia.<ref name="Operation S.I.N.">''Operation S.I.N.'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> The three of them worked to keep the alien from being taken by [[Hydra (comics)|Hydra]] and had the alien live with [[Crimson Dynamo|Anton Vanko]].<ref>''Operation S.I.N.'' #2-5. Marvel Comics.</ref>

In the 1960s, Peggy Carter joined up with [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] for a long tenure.<ref>''Agent Carter: S.H.I.E.L.D. Anniversary'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref>

At the time when Captain America resurfaced in the world, Peggy Carter was taking treatments from [[Doctor Faustus (comics)|Doctor Faustus]]. When she was rescued by Captain America, she maintained her friendship with him.<ref>''Captain America'' #161-163. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Peggy Carter later helped Captain America fight the [[Secret Empire (organization)|Secret Empire]].<ref>''Captain America'' #174-175. Marvel Comics.</ref> She also dealt with his decision to stop being Captain America for a while.<ref>''Captain America'' #176. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Peggy helps Captain America when her niece Sharon Carter and some S.H.I.E.L.D. Agents went missing while gathering information on the new Grand Director.<ref>''Captain America'' #231-236. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Peggy Carter later joined the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]]' support staff at [[Avengers Mansion]].<ref>''Avengers'' #300. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During her retirement, Peggy lived in a nursing home and started suffering dementia where she couldn't recognize Sharon. William Burnside later visited Peggy claiming to be the original Captain America.<ref>''Captain America'' Vol. 5 #45. Marvel Comics.</ref> Peggy Carter passed away as Steve Rogers, [[Nick Fury]], and [[Dum Dum Dugan]] attended her funeral.<ref name="Operation S.I.N."/> S.H.I.E.L.D. erected a memorial statue of her outside the S.H.I.E.L.D. Academy in Newark.<ref>''Silk'' Vol. 2 #8. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Through unknown means, Peggy Carter was revived and rejuvenated where she joined Sharon in forming the Daughters of Liberty under the alias of Dryad. She assisted the group in working to clear Captain America's name when he was framed for the death of [[Thunderbolt Ross]].<ref>''Captain America'' Vol. 9 #9-11. Marvel Comics.</ref> Captain America learned of her identity when Dryad unmasked herself.<ref>''Captain America'' Vol. 9 #12. Marvel Comics.</ref>

==Abilities==
Peggy Carter is shown to be a superb hand-to-hand combatant and martial artist. She excels in using firearms and is an experienced tactician and commander.

==Other versions==
* In the alternate reality created by Scarlet Witch in the 2005 "[[House of M]]" storyline, Captain America is never frozen in the Arctic, and instead marries Peggy shortly after World War II ends.<ref name="CAv5no10">''[[Captain America]]'' vol. 5 #10 (Oct. 2005). Marvel Comics.</ref>
* On [[Spider-Gwen|Earth-65]], Peggy Carter is the long-lived director of S.H.I.E.L.D., much like Nick Fury in the primary universe. She also sports an eye patch similar to the one worn by Fury.<ref>''Spider-Gwen'' #2</ref>
* In ''Super-Soldier: Man of War'' #1 from [[Amalgam Comics]], '''Mademoiselle Peggy''' is a cross between Peggy Carter and [[DC Comics]]' World War II version of [[Mademoiselle Marie]].<ref>''Super-Soldier: Man of War'' #1 (June 1997). Amalgam Comics.</ref>
