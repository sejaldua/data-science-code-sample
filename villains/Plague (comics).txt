'''Plague''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. She was originally a member of the [[Morlocks (comics)|Morlocks]] before joining the [[Horsemen of Apocalypse]].

==Publication history==
Plague first appeared in ''[[Uncanny X-Men]]'' #169-170 (May-June 1983), and was created by [[Chris Claremont]] and [[Paul Smith (comics)|Paul Smith]].

The character subsequently appears as '''Pestilence''' in ''X-Factor'' #10 (November 1986), #15 (April 1987), #17 (June 1987), #19 (August 1987), #22-25 (November 1987-February 1988), ''Power Pack'' (February 1988), and ''Daredevil'' #252 (March 1988.

Plague appeared as part of the "Morlocks" entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]'' #9.

==Fictional character biography==
Plague is an old woman that was a member of a large community of mutants who felt they were outcasts who live in the underground tunnels beneath New York, New Jersey, and Connecticut known as the [[Morlocks (comics)|Morlocks]].{{Issue|date=August 2011}}

Plague was present during the Morlock [[Mutant Massacre|massacre]] by the [[Marauders (comics)|Marauders]] and was terrorized by [[Sabretooth (comics)|Sabretooth]], who was unaware of her abilities and left weakened and might have died if not for his healing factor.{{Issue|date=August 2011}} [[Apocalypse (comics)|Apocalypse]] arrived and saved Plague from the massacre, stating that he had plans for her to serve him as the Horseman Pestilence.<ref name="X-Factor_10">''X-Factor'' #10</ref>

After being transformed into Pestilence, Plague, [[War (Marvel Comics character)|War]], and [[Famine (comics)|Famine]] battled [[X-Factor (comics)|X-Factor]] in Central Park and were defeated by [[Iceman (comics)|Iceman]], but not before they killed countless humans.<ref name="X-Factor_10" /> After the fourth Horseman, [[Warren Worthington|Death]], was revealed, Apocalypse had the Horsemen battle each other, to find the leader and Death beat them all.{{Issue|date=August 2011}}

After Apocalypse teleported X-Factor on his Celestial Ship, they battled the Horsemen again and, after their defeat, the Horsemen were sent into Manhattan to incite chaos.{{Issue|date=August 2011}} X-Factor and the Power Pack battled the Horsemen and Lightspeed knocked Pestilence off her flying steed, whereupon she fell to her death.<ref>''Power Pack'' #35</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Plague's immune system made her essentially immune to all known diseases. However, she could be exposed to a disease, alter it to be more lethal, and then spread it by touch. Her power was augmented by Apocalypse to an unknown degree.{{Issue|date=January 2012}}

As Pestilence, she also utilized an electric flying robotic steed constructed by Apocalypse's Ship.{{Issue|date=January 2012}}

==In other media==
===Television===
* Plague first makes a cameo in the ''[[X-Men (TV series)|X-Men]]'' episode "[[Captive Hearts (X-Men)|Captive Hearts]]", voiced by [[Judy Marshak]]. She appears as one of the Morlocks fighting the X-Men and incapacitating Gambit. She returns in the episode "[[Come the Apocalypse]]" wanting a cure and is eventually transformed into the Horseman Pestilence.
