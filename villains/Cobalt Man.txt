'''Cobalt Man''' is a [[fictional character|fictional]] [[supervillain]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Cobalt Man was created by writer [[Roy Thomas]] and penciller  [[Werner Roth (comics)|Werner Roth]] in ''[[X-Men]]'' #31 (April 1967).

The character subsequently appeared in ''X-Men'' #34 (July 1967) and #79 (Dec. 1973), ''Hulk'' vol. 2, #173-175 (March–May 1974), ''The Defenders'' #42-43 (Dec. 1976-Jan. 1977), ''Hulk: Nightmerica'' #1 (Aug. 2003), ''Avengers/Thunderbolts'' #1 (May 2004), and ''Civil War'' #1 (July 2006), in which he died. He appeared posthumously in ''Marvel Spotlight Civil War: Aftermath'' #1 (March 2007).

A [[Skrull]] version of Cobalt Man appears in the “[[Secret Invasion]]” storyline in ''Captain Marvel'' #2-3 (Feb.–March 2008).

Cobalt Man received an entry in the ''Marvel Legacy: The 1970s Handbook'' #1 (2006).

==Fictional character biography==
===Ralph Roberts===
'''Ralph Roberts''' was an inventor studying the effects of [[nuclear radiation]] involving [[cobalt]]. Seeking to weaponize this, he built a cobalt suit of armor in imitation of [[Iron Man]]'s, and called himself Cobalt Man. At the time, Ralph's younger brother Ted Roberts was dating [[Jean Grey]], secretly of the mutant superhero group the [[X-Men]]. Ralph took Ted, Jean, and [[Cyclops (Marvel Comics)|Scott Summers]] on a tour of his lab. He showed Ted his armor and became unstable and destructive. The X-Men defeated him, and he realized the armor was too dangerous to use.<ref>''The X-Men'' #31</ref> However, the subterranean despot [[Tyrannus (comics)|Tyrannus]] soon kidnapped him to force him to build a giant cobalt robot. The X-Men fought Tyrannus and rescued Roberts and returned him to the surface world.<ref>''The X-Men'' #34</ref>

He later achieved superhuman size and power due to radiation that slowly begins killing him; addled, he attempts to show the world the dangers of nuclear power by destroying himself and the city of [[Sydney, Australia]]. In a confrontation with the Hulk, he seemingly dies, safely away from the Earth.<ref>''Hulk'' vol. 2, #174-175 (May–April 1974)</ref> Later, while on a mission with [[Egghead (Marvel Comics)|Egghead]]'s [[Emissaries of Evil]], he appears to have been killed in a [[nuclear explosion]],<ref>''Defenders'' #42-43</ref> but in actuality survived.<ref>''Hulk: Nightmerica'' #1</ref>

[[image:A Skrull posing as Cobalt Man.jpg|thumb|left|A Skrull posing as Cobalt Man.<br>Art by [[Lee Weeks]].]]
Cobalt Man was one of the many supervillains who escaped from the prison The Raft.<ref>''New Avengers'' #1-3 (Jan.-March 2005)</ref> The superhero team the [[New Warriors]], stars of a [[reality-TV]] show, located Cobalt Man hiding in [[Stamford, Connecticut]] with other escaped convicts.<ref>''New Avengers: Illuminati'' #1 (May 2006) but which was originally produced as part of ''Civil War'' #1 (July 2006)</ref> A powerful explosion during the ensuing conflict killed hundreds of people, apparently including most of the New Warriors and Cobalt Man.<ref>''Civil War'' #1</ref>

Ralph Roberts seemingly returned from the dead in the 2007-08 ''Captain Marvel'' miniseries, this proved to be a [[Skrull]] impostor.<ref>''Captain Marvel'' (vol. 7) #1</ref>

During the “[[Dark Reign (comics)|Dark Reign]]” storyline, [[Hercules (Marvel Comics)|Hercules]] travels to the Underworld and finds Cobalt Man among the dead in [[Erebus]]. There Cobalt Man and the others have a chance of returning to life by winning at gambling.<ref>''Incredible Hercules'' #129 (2009)</ref>

Cobalt Man somehow reappears alive and retired in ''Deadpool & the Mercs for Money'', which has the eponymous characters capture him at the behest of a company called Umbral Dynamics.<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Cullen Bunn|penciller = Iban Coello|inker = Iban Coello|colorist = Guru-eFX|letterer = VC's Joe Sabino|editor = Heather Antos and Jordan D. White|title = Deadpool & the Mercs for Money|volume = 2|issue = #3|date = Sept. 7, 2016|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref>

===Tony Stark as Cobalt Man===
During the 2004 ''Avengers/Thunderbolts'' six issue mini-series, [[Iron Man|Tony Stark]] infiltrated the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]] as Cobalt Man to learn their intentions and agenda. Eventually, Stark revealed his true identity and tossed the Cobalt Man armor away.<ref>''Avengers/Thunderbolts'' #1-6</ref>

===Cobalt Men===
A group of men in Cobalt Man armor attacked the superhero [[Robbie Baldwin|Speedball]] and a group of trainee Avengers when they visited Stamford, Connecticut. A local police officer revealed the group was pro-[[Norman Osborn]] fanatics who had fabricated copies of the Cobalt Man armor with plans to make the heroes look bad in public.<ref>''Avengers Academy'' #10 (March 2011)</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Initially, Ralph Roberts wore the Cobalt Man armor to gain Iron Man-like abilities, including flight, superhuman strength (it multiplied his strength 100x) and durability, recoil beams, etc. The armor (at least the second version) also had oxygen tanks and could protect its wearer from the vacuum of space.

==In other media==
===Television===
* A version of Cobalt Man appears in ''[[Avengers Assemble (TV series)|Avengers: Ultron Revolution]]''.{{cn|date=September 2016}} This iteration is a robot with the ability to teleport, and presumably created by [[Ultron]]. In the episode "A Friend in Need", he fights the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] by teleporting away from attacks until the [[Vision (Marvel Comics)|Vision]] arrives. Vision is able to disable Cobalt Man's teleportation device enough for the Avengers to defeat the android.
