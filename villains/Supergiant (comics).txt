'''Supergiant''' is a supervillain appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Supergiant first appeared in ''[[Infinity (comic book)|Infinity]]'' #1 (October [[2013 in comics|2013]]) and was created by [[Jonathan Hickman]] and [[Jerome Opeña]].<ref>https://www.cbr.com/exclusive-introducing-the-black-order-of-infinity-part-three/</ref>

==Fictional character biography==
Supergiant is a member of [[Thanos]]' [[Black Order (comics)|Black Order]]. She is a mentally unstable omnipath and telepathic parasite who seeks out intellect to devour.<ref>''Infinity'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref>

In the search for Thanos' son [[Thane (comics)|Thane]] during the ''[[Infinity (comic book)|Infinity]]'' storyline, Supergiant and [[Corvus Glaive]] laid siege to the [[X-Mansion|Jean Grey School for Higher Learning]] and easily defeated the [[X-Men]]. They left after realizing that Thane was not there.<ref>''New Avengers'' vol. 3 #9. Marvel Comics.</ref>

When the Black Order seized [[Wakanda]], Supergiant was left in control of [[Black Bolt]] who she would mentally order to activate the [[Illuminati (comics)|Illuminati]]'s hidden bombs in Wakanda's Necropolis.<ref>''Infinity'' #5</ref> When the Illuminati arrived to save Black Bolt, Supergiant used Black Bolt's ability to defeat the heroes. Upon activating the bomb, Supergiant was faced by [[Maximus (comics)|Maximus]] who had the trigger. Maximus triggered the bomb, but also used [[Lockjaw (comics)|Lockjaw]] to transport Supergiant along with the bomb to a distant uninhabited planet where she perished in the explosion.<ref>''Infinity'' #6. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the "No Surrender" arc, Supergiant came back as a psychic projection, along with the rest of the Black Order, thanks to the [[Challenger (comics)|Challenger]] who had set them up against [[Grandmaster (Marvel Comics)|Grandmaster]]'s [[Lethal Legion]].<ref>''Avengers'' #676. Marvel Comics.</ref> During the battle in Antarctica, the Black Order retreat after [[Corvus Glaive]] is killed. Supergiant stays to take control of [[Jane Foster (comics)|Thor]], but is dissipated by Lethal Legion member Ferene the Other.<ref>''The Avengers'' #680. Marvel Comics</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Supergiant has telepathic abilities that allow her to mind-control anyone and feed on their intellect.
