'''Hellrazor''' is a [[fictional]] [[assassination|assassin]] character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

== First Hellrazor ==

===Publication history===
Hellrazor first appeared in ''[[Marvel Team-Up]]'' #87 (November 1979), and was created by [[Steven Grant]] and [[Gene Colan]].

The character subsequently appears in ''Captain America'' #319 (July 1986), in which he was killed by the [[Scourge of the Underworld]].

===Fictional character biography===
Hellrazor was a costumed criminal outfitted by [[Roxxon Oil]] Corporation, hired to defame and kill the [[Black Panther (comics)|Black Panther]]. He was defeated by the combined forces of T’Challa and [[Spider-Man]].<ref>''Marvel Team-Up'' #87</ref>

Hellrazor was caught without his weapons at the "Bar With No Name," and shot dead by the [[Scourge of the Underworld]].<ref>{{cite comic|writer = [[Mark Gruenwald]]|penciller = [[Paul Neary]]|inker = [[Dennis Janke]]|story = Overkill|title = [[Captain America]]|volume = 1|issue = 319|date = July 1986|publisher = Marvel Comics}}</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Hellrazor can draw upon any energy source to replenish his strength and stamina.

Hellrazor’s primary weapons were a pair of wrist blades – bracelets which had sharpened edges, and could also fire a stream of sharp razors from his wrists.

==Violator==
===Civil War===
Another character named Hellrazor has appeared as a [[Registration acts (comics)#2006 Superhuman Registration Act|Superhuman Registration Act]] violator. He was running a protection racket in Billings, Montana, and was being held as a prisoner of the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]]. Two days after his arrest, he encountered [[Robbie Baldwin|Penance]].  Hellrazor began to taunt Penance about his role in the Stamford incident, until Penance attacked him and beat him severely.<ref>''Thunderbolts'' #116</ref>

It is unknown at this time what connection, if any, he has to the Caprice, [[Mind-Wave#Caged Angels|Mindwave]], [[Mirage (Marvel Comics)#Caged Angels|Mirage]], and [[Blue Streak (comics)#Caged Angels|Bluestreak]], who were also held as prisoners of the Thunderbolts shortly thereafter.
