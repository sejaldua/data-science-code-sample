'''Tempo''' is a [[fictional]] [[African-American]] [[Mutant (Marvel Comics)|mutant]] character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. She was first introduced as a member of the [[Mutant Liberation Front]] in ''[[New Mutants]]'' (February 1990).

==Publication history==
Tempo first appeared in the comic ''[[New Mutants]]'' issue #86, although in her first cameo appearance she is incorrectly referred to as "[[Strobe (comics)|Strobe]]", which is actually the name of her teammate.{{Citation needed|date=December 2016}} Her debut appearance is made in issue #87 of ''New Mutants'' in March 1990.

==Fictional character biography==
Tempo (alter ego Heather Tucker) is a founding member of the [[terrorist]] organization known as the Mutant Liberation Front (MLF). Tempo is a mainstay in the team's adventures, including fighting [[Wolverine (character)|Wolverine]], [[Sunfire (comics)|Sunfire]], [[X-Factor (comics)|X-Factor]] and others in the name of mutant rights, using her time-bending powers.{{Issue|date=February 2011}}

The MLF destroy the Tucker Clinic, an institute for pregnant mothers to undergo [[prenatal]] [[DNA]] testing to determine whether or not their babies would be mutants, resulting in the death of Dr. Tucker. The story hints that Tempo may have been his daughter.<ref>''X-Factor'' #78 (May 1992)</ref>

Soon after, a tyrannical despot named [[Reignfire]] reforms the MLF and tasks them with killing [[Henry Peter Gyrich]]. Tempo intervenes to save Gyrich and is exiled from the MLF.{{Issue|date=February 2011}}

After leaving the MLF, [[Cable (comics)|Cable]] invites Tempo to join X-Force but she declines, choosing instead to go to college and put aside the super-powered lifestyle.{{issue|date=January 2013}} She is again briefly affiliated with the MLF during ''[[Operation: Zero Tolerance]]''.{{issue|date=January 2013}} <ref>''X-Force'' #67 (June 1997)</ref>

After [[Decimation (comics)|M-Day]], Tempo is one of the few to retain her powers. She then becomes a member of the reformed [[Acolytes (comics)|Acolytes]].{{Issue|date=February 2011}} When they disband she joins Utopia and works alongside the X-Men.{{Issue|date=February 2011}}

In [[Age of X]], Tempo is transformed into a soldier of Fortress X.<ref>{{cite web|author= George A. Tramountanas |url=http://www.cbr.com/x-position-mike-carey-on-the-age-of-x-aftermath/ |title=X-POSITION: Mike Carey on the "Age of X" Aftermath |publisher=CBR |date=2011-06-21 |accessdate=2016-12-12}}</ref> Tempo is fatally wounded while battling humans, but before she dies [[Rogue (comics)|Legacy]] absorbs her memories.<ref>''X-Men Legacy'' #245</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Tempo can manipulate time. She is able to slow or stop her opponents and speed up either herself or her teammates. She has prevented grenades from exploding by keeping them in suspended motion, and was also able to successfully attack [[Rogue (comics)|Rogue]] by amplifying Rogue's speed, causing her to crash into a wall while flying.

Strain limits how long Tempo can maintain a major time manipulation. While it was never established how long that limit is, she was able to completely freeze the X-Men for several minutes while she and the Acolytes conducted a thorough search of the X-Men mansion.<ref>''X-Men'' #202 (Oct. 2007)</ref> Tempo can also levitate herself and fly at subsonic speeds.{{Issue|date=January 2013}}

Besides offering her physical protection, Tempo's helmet also contains electronic equipment that provides her with resistance to [[Cable (comics)|Cable]]'s telepathic probes.<ref>''X-Factor'' #78 (May 1992)</ref>
