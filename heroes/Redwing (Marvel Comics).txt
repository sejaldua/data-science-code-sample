'''Redwing''' is a [[character (arts)|fictional bird]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. Primarily a supporting character of [[Falcon (comics)|Falcon]], the character exists within Marvel's main [[shared universe]], known as the [[Marvel Universe]].

Redwing appears as a drone in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] films ''[[Ant-Man (film)|Ant-Man]]'', ''[[Captain America: Civil War]]'', and ''[[Avengers: Infinity War]].''

==Publication history==
{{expand-section|date=October 2016}}
Redwing debuted alongside the Falcon in ''Captain America'' #117 and was created by [[Stan Lee]] and [[Gene Colan]].

==Fictional character biography==
{{expand section|date=November 2016}}
In [[Rio de Janeiro]], [[Falcon (comics)|Sam Wilson]] encounters a red feathered falcon that he names Redwing. The Nazi [[Red Skull]] uses the [[Cosmic Cube]] to connect Sam and Redwing by a [[Telepathy|mental link]]. Wilson and Redwing then become allies of [[Captain America]].<ref>''Captain America'' #186</ref> Redwing has been by Falcon's side ever since, fighting villains such as [[Grey Gargoyle]], [[Viper (Madame Hydra)|Viper]] and [[Diamondhead (comics)|Diamondhead]]. Redwing stayed with Falcon when he became the new Captain America.<ref>''All-New Captain America'' #1</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
In his earliest appearances Redwing exhibits a close bond with Sam Wilson, which is confirmed as being a telepathic link by [[Professor X]] in ''Captain America'' #174 . The [[Red Skull]] later reveals that he had used the [[Cosmic Cube]] to create a "super-normal mental link" between Sam Wilson and Redwing.<ref>Englehart, Steve. ''Captain America'' #186. Marvel Comics, 1975</ref> Falcon later recalled the experience, stating "Hurt like ''hell''. Being mentally ''fused'' with that falcon. Able to ''see'' through ''his'' eyes."<ref>Johns, Geoff. ''Avengers'' vol. 3 #60. Marvel Comics, 2003</ref>

===Video Games===
Redwing also appears in ''[[Lego Marvel's Avengers]]'' and ''[[Marvel Contest of Champions]]''. In both games, he is not a playable character, but serves as Falcon's projectile attack, in which he flies towards the selected targets before coming back to Falcon.
