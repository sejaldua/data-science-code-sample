'''Al MacKenzie''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

Alphonso "Mack" Mackenzie appears in the TV series ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'', portrayed by [[Henry Simmons]].

==Publication history==
Al MacKenzie first appeared in ''[[Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'' #3 (August 1988), and was created by [[Bob Harras]] and [[Paul Neary]].

The character subsequently appears in ''[[Nick Fury, Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D. (comic book)|Nick Fury, Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'' #1-7 (September 1989-January 1990).

An entry for Al MacKenzie appears in issue four of the reference series ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Update '89]]''.

==Fictional character biography==
Al MacKenzie was born in [[Austin, Texas]]. He was once the [[Central Intelligence Agency|C.I.A.]] liaison to [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]]<ref>''Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe'' (Vol 3) #4. Marvel Comics (New York).</ref> He became romantically involved with Contessa [[Valentina Allegra di Fontaine]] after a while, which led to an estrangement between him and [[Nick Fury]].<ref>''Nick Fury, Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D.'' (Vol. 3) #1. Marvel Comics (New York).</ref> Because of this, he returned to the CIA with the Contessa.<ref>''Nick Fury, Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D.'' (Vol. 3) #3. Marvel Comics (New York).</ref>

MacKenzie later joined the S.H.I.E.L.D. organization full-time, and spent considerable time as the senior liaison officer to the C.I.A.<ref>''Nick Fury, Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D.'' (Vol. 3) #6. Marvel Comics (New York).</ref> Subsequently, under unknown circumstances, MacKenzie resigned from S.H.I.E.L.D., and wrote a "tell-all" book entitled ''UnSHIELDed: an Unauthorized Insider's Look Behind the World's Most Powerful Global Spy Network'', which purportedly explained some of the history behind the organization from his point of view.<ref>''[[Amazing Fantasy]]'' Vol. 2, # 10. Marvel Comics (New York).</ref> Since then, he also acted as an unofficial source to [[Ben Urich]], prodding Urich and [[Jessica Jones]] to attempt to expose Fury's unauthorized mission to [[Latveria]].<ref>''[[The Pulse (comics)|The Pulse]]'' # 9. Marvel Comics (New York).</ref>

==In other media==
[[image:Al_MacKenzie_Agents_of_SHIELD_S3_E22.png|thumb|right|[[Henry_Simmons]] as Al MacKenzie in ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'']]
Mackenzie appears in the TV series ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'', portrayed by [[Henry Simmons]]. His full name is [[Mack (Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.)|Alphonso MacKenzie]], but goes by the nickname "Mack". He is an African-American [[mechanic]] and [[engineer]] who is recruited into the newly rebuilt S.H.I.E.L.D. by [[Phil Coulson]] after the events of the film ''[[Captain America: The Winter Soldier]]''. He is old friends with [[Mockingbird (Marvel Comics)|Barbara "Bobbi" Morse]].<ref>{{cite episode|title=[[One of Us (Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.)|One of Us]]|series=Marvel's Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.|credits=Tancharoen, Kevin (director); Monica Owusu-Breen (writer)|network=[[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]|airdate=March 17, 2015|season=2|number=13}}</ref> He develops a close friendship with [[Leo Fitz]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://comics2film.com/marvel-s-agents-of-s-h-i-e-l-d-fitz-mack-s-bromance|title=Marvel's Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. {{!}} Fitz + Mack's Bromance|publisher=Comics2Film|date=July 31, 2019|accessdate=August 12, 2019|archivedate=August 12, 2019|archiveurl=http://archive.is/o3AiW}}</ref> and a romantic relationship with  [[Yo-Yo Rodriguez|Elena "Yo-Yo" Rodriguez]].<ref>{{cite episode|title=[[Bouncing Back]]|series=Marvel's Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.|credits=Underwood, Ron (director); Monica Owusu-Breen (writer)|network=ABC|airdate=March 8, 2016|season=3|number=11}}</ref> Mack later goes on to become the director of S.H.I.E.L.D due to the ill health and subsequent death of [[Phil Coulson]].

Simmons reprises his role in a six part [[web series]] titled ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.: Slingshot]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.comingsoon.net/tv/trailers/792743-agents-of-shield-lmd-and-a-slingshot-digital-series-are-coming#/slide/1|title=Agents of SHIELD: LMD and a Slingshot Digital Series are Coming|author=CS|website=[[ComingSoon.net]]|date=December 6, 2016|accessdate=December 7, 2016}}</ref>
